# The Shocking Cost of Youth Sports in New Jersey
**Source:** NJ.com | Steve Politi | May 28, 2026
**URL:** https://www.nj.com/sports/2026/05/the-shocking-cost-of-youth-sports-in-new-jersey.html

## Key Statistics
- **Cost increase:** 46% since 2019 (Aspen Institute study)
- **Industry size:** $40 billion
- **Attrition rate:** 2/3 of kids quit sports by age 13
- **Average travel team cost:** $3,000/year (Aspen Institute - "laughably low" per article)
- **Reported realistic costs:** $10,000-$30,000+/year

## Root Causes Identified

### System Failures
- Budget cuts since Great Recession eliminated rec programs
- Towns (Jefferson, Montclair, Nutley, West Orange, Lacey) considering eliminating rec/HS sports entirely
- Private equity filling the void

### FOMO-Driven Model
- Social media showcasing elite programs
- "Bullet train" mentality: must go all-in early or foreclose opportunity
- Fear parents won't measure up to peers

### "Nickel and Diming"
- $20 tickets to watch grade-school games
- $25 tryout fees for teams kids are already on
- $50 streaming service fees for highlights
- Hotel mandates for tournament participation
- Tournament hosting fees
- Private lessons ($100+/session)

### Private Equity Impact
- **Unrivaled Sports** (Devils co-owners Harris/Blitzer) bought Diamond Nation (Flemington)
- **Black Bear Sports Group** (Wall Street-backed) owns 9 NJ ice rinks, forced local clubs out
- **NJ Titans** netted ~$8M revenue (2024)
- **Players Development Academy** netted ~$8M revenue (2024)

## Key Insights

### College Recruitment Reality
- **College scholarship:** "Fool's gold for most families"
- **Better investment:** SAT prep classes and 529 college savings accounts
- **NIL myth:** Median NIL deal is $72 (less than McDonald's family meal)

### Equity Crisis
- Sports once "great equalizer" - now province of the privileged
- Lower-income families priced out entirely
- Suburban families with resources get best training

### Time Burden
- Weeknight practices (1+ hour commute each way)
- Weekends dominated by tournaments
- Vacations built around events
- "Running them ragged" on unnecessary events

### The Resignation
- Experts describe "youth sports crisis"
- Even reform efforts ("Children's Bill of Rights") meet resistance
- "Too much money invested in the system to tweak it" — John O'Sullivan

## Policy Response

### Sen. Cory Booker (NJ)
- **Legislation:** "Let Kids Play Act"
- **Goal:** Ban private equity from youth sports
- **Model:** Comparison to airline industry (baggage fees, seat charges)
- **Quote:** "I want to turn agonizing into mobilizing"

### Project Play (Dan Farrey)
- **Strategy:** Use facility permits to prioritize kid-focused organizations
- **Bill of Rights:** Focus on enjoyment vs winning for young kids
- **Challenge:** "Not irreversible, but resistance is strong"
