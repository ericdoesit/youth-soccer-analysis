The Real Math Behind Club Soccer

The data is out there. It's just never been collected in one place. Help change that.

What We Think

The story parents are told on the sideline:

  • Elite clubs give kids the best shot at a college scholarship
  • Starting young and investing more leads to better outcomes
  • If everyone else is doing it, we have to
  • The investment pays off. It's for their future.
What We Know

The numbers that rarely make it to the sideline:

  • Youth sports is a $40 billion industry, up 46% since 2019
  • Odds of a D1 full scholarship for a competitive 15-year-old: less than 1%
  • Typical elite club spend over 6 years: $54,000+, before travel, privates, and tournaments
  • That same $54,000 in a college savings account grows to $126,000 by freshman year
  • Two-thirds of kids quit organized sports by age 13. Burnout and cost are the top reasons.
  • NCAA D1 men's soccer offers 9.9 scholarships per team, split among 20+ players. Most "scholarships" cover less than a semester.
"If you're chasing a scholarship, just know that you're crazy." — Jon Simoneau, teacher, coach, and soccer parent • NJ.com, May 2026
What We Don't Know

The data that doesn't exist yet. Clubs won't share it:

  • What families are actually spending, all-in, by region and level
  • How many competitive players eventually earn any scholarship at all
  • Whether elite clubs deliver better outcomes than lower-cost alternatives
  • How much of the financial burden falls on families earning under $100k
